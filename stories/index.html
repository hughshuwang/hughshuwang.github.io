In the Tokyo vlog by edocolala, Cola was saying:
"Even in such a huge, busy city, we can't really speed up,
we just wander around, use cameras to record beautiful moments.
There's no plan for everyday's travel, we just follow our feelings.
That day on the beach, all we remember is just the nice weather,
and feeling absolutely comfortable with each other.
Everyone is rushing, but we still try to slow it down."

Simply watching this made me cry.
I was rushing my life too much,
way too much.

A good function should never be more than 50 lines
A good project should never contain a "rotten" function
Always draft and clearify the system on paper before coding
Always be prepared for massive structual changes
Always avoid stinking loops and redundant variables
Always think both as a mathematician and an engineer

- a short note on personal view of elegant problem solving

It's the video of Shaun White winning the half pipe gold in Olympics 2018
Full 100 seconds close-up before his epic performance, the host was saying
"Shaun White, the biggest run in his life right now."
I can see his eyes, his face, as he's preparing for the last run.
All of a sudden, I got the whole idea of it:

In all of the greatest moments by the greatest people,
the underlying core is not mysterious or complicated at all.
All they had in mind was just a simple will, a straight focus.
The hardness and toughness only happened behind the scene.
We will never know.

Everyone has something to respect, admire, and love.
Everything has something to thank, appreciate, and cherish.
Live is self-salvation, not self-abasement or self-abandonment.
To be good to yourself is the only advice.

Nobody will win forever and nobody will lose forever.
We all have our own time zones, there's no such a thing called falling behind.
The gods have special paths prepared for each of us; no rush.
The greedy illusion is our only weakness.

- a slice from the list of personal values

3 A.M., someday in November 2012, Peking University, Beijing, China
The last night before leaving Beijing, heading to the CPhO national final in Jilin.
Ydz, who's also a national finalist in CPhO 3 years prior to me, was at Tsinghua
earning his Bachelor's degree in computer science and microelectronics,
ended his speech with the last sentence:

"If you know you CAN do it,
you MUST do it RIGHT."

He's the one who inspired me to appreciate science and technology
in a way that's the same as how we appreciate sex
