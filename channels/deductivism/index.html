Bio

My research interests include empirical asset pricing, portfolio optimization, statistical learning, and mathematical programming. After graduation, I have been staying in Cambridge and working for BlackRock/55 Institutional as a researcher for almost two years. Supervised by Prof. Leonid Kogan, I’m currently focusing on large-scale online MIP and AI applications to improve the multibillion-dollar flagship iShares ETF-based BlackRock Model Portfolios, specifically, the tactical asset allocation solutions under active tax management, including tax-efficient rebalancing, tax-loss-harvesting, and tax-aware model transition, for SMAs and households.

I received my Master’s degree in finance/financial mathematics from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and my Bachelor’s degree in finance from Jiao Tong University. I also spent part of my college life at University of California, Berkeley and Stanford University as an exchange student and a certificated research trainee.
