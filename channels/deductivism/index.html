Intro

My research interests include mathematical programming, statistical learning, empirical asset pricing, and multi-asset allocation. After graduation in 2018, I’ve been working for BlackRock/55 Institutional as a researcher in quantitative analysis and portfolio construction. Supervised by Prof. Leonid Kogan, I’m mainly focusing on large-scale mixed-integer programming (MIP) and ML/AI applications to enhance the multibillion-dollar BlackRock® Model Portfolios, the iShares®-based tactical asset allocation solution for financial advisors under systematic active tax and risk management.

Before getting into the asset management industry, I received my Master’s degree in finance/financial mathematics from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and my Bachelor’s degree in finance from Jiao Tong University, Shanghai. I also spent part of my college life at University of California, Berkeley and Stanford University as an exchange student and a certificated research trainee. Prior to college, I was a national finalist in Chinese Physics Olympiad (CPhO) and made it top 150 in China, which got me pre-accepted without attending the college entrance examination. 😄
