Intro

My research interests include mathematical programming, statistical learning, empirical asset pricing, and multi-asset allocation. After graduation in 2018, I’ve been working for BlackRock/55 Institutional as a researcher in quantitative analysis and portfolio construction. Supervised by Prof. Leonid Kogan, I’m mainly focusing on large-scale mixed-integer programming (MIP) and ML/AI applications to enhance the multibillion-dollar BlackRock® Model Portfolios, the ETF-based tactical asset allocation solution for financial advisors under systematic active tax and risk management.

Before getting into the asset management industry, I received my Master’s degree in finance/financial mathematics from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and my Bachelor’s degree in finance from Jiao Tong University, Shanghai. I also spent part of my college life at University of California, Berkeley and Stanford University as an exchange student and a certificated research trainee. Prior to college, I was a national finalist in Chinese Physics Olympiad (CPhO) and made it top 150 in China, which got me pre-accepted without attending the college entrance examination.

I’m a Vim fanatic - key remaps and emulators are everywhere in my computing units for ultimate effectiveness. I have three keyboards, all of which are great exemplars of simplism and compactness: an iKBC New Poker II with Cherry red switches, a glorious pure black blank HHKB Pro 2, and a pure white Apple magic keyboard. I use Visual Studio Code with Vim bindings as my major dev tool, and I code in Python, R, and Julia, also play with KDB+/Q, MySQL, LaTeX, html/css/javascript, and vim/shell scripts sometimes.

Inspired by the book “The Pragmatic Programmer”, I keep myself exposed in multiple operating systems as much as possible. In my daily work, I use the elegant Ubuntu 18.04 LTS Bionic Beaver under WSL and the ubiquitous Windows 10 Pro; my personal laptop is an early-2015 old fashioned 13-inch MacBook Pro running macOS 10.14 Mojave. My terminal experience is powered by zsh/oh-my-zsh and ranger - both are masterpieces born in the vigorous GNU/Linux community.
