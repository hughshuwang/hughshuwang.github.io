Preface

Welcome. I’m Shu. Glad you came.

I believe in Occam’s Razor, so I tried to build this site in the simplest way possible, and only show the essentials for you to know about myself, all the cool things I’m into, and, hopefully, to discover any resemblance between us, so that we could be friends.

You might ask, why this name “CYANED” instead of a more straightforward “The Portfolio of Shu Shean”? It’s simple, cyan, a greenish blue, is my favourite color. As mentioned in Wikipedia, it’s the color of shallow water over a sandy beach, and it’s the color of Uranus. The ‘ed’ suffix comes from the name of the official and record label of Frank Ocean (BLONDED.CO) - he’s the legend I always look up to, the inspiration of all my creativity, the one taught me how to be myself and just don’t give a shit.

Life is short, so I spend my time wisely, and look forward to experiencing different forms of “being a human”: as a mathematician, an engineer, a programmer, a dancer, a singer, a photographer, a novelist, a guitar player, a producer, a traveler, a snowboarder, and so many others. I’m actively exploring my interests and refuse to settle with anything, even those I’ve been always good at.

Now, maybe it’s better to spread things out a bit so you can walk around on this spacy canvas and see if anything catches you.









Music

Music has always been the backbone of my energy - I need good music much more than a morning coffee shot. I listen to a wide range of music, from the old-school Jazz from Keith Jarrett to the most aggressive experimental from Arca, but most of the time I focus mainly on Contemporary R&B, Dream Pop, and Alternative Rock, which I also spend a lot of time studying and creating.

I was raised in a family of western pop music fans - in my dad’s study room there are tons of vinyl records and cds by Michael Jackson, Madonna, The Beetles, and Eagles, and on the CD player screen of his old Mercedes S-class, the legendary Bucharest Concert had been played more than hundreds of times, if not thousands.
